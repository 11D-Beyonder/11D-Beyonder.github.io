A. Portal ↬\looparrowright↬

题目描述

You are now in a big factory. The factory could be recognized as a graph with n vertices and m edges. Every edge has its length. You have kkk missions to do. The iii-th mission is going to vertex aia_iai​, picking a block and then sending it to vertex bib_ibi​. You should complete the missions in the order from 111-st to kkk-th. Initially you are standing at vertex 111.
You have a gun in your hand. When you are at some vertex uuu, you could shoot the gun at the ground, and then a portal will be built at vertex uuu. When there are two portals in the factory, assuming they are at uuu and vvv, you could transfer between uuu and vvv with no cost (just like an edge connecting uuu and vvv with length 000).
You also have a remote controller in your hand. It allows you to close a portal whenever you want and wherever you are (closing one portal at a time, not all portals at the same time). What’s more, there could be at most two existing portals. So if you want to create another portal when there exists two, you must use your controller to close one before you create.
You need to find the minimum distance you have to walk in order to complete all kkk missions.

D. Drop Voicing ↬\looparrowright↬

题目描述

Inaka composes music. Today’s arrangement includes a chord of nnn notes that are pairwise distinct, represented by a permutation p1,p2,⋯ ,pnp_1,p_2,\cdots,p_np1​,p2​,⋯,pn​ of integers from 111 to nnn (inclusive) denoting the notes from the lowest to the highest.
Her friend, Miyako, sneaks in and plays a trick by altering the chord with the following two operations. Drop-2\text{Drop-2}Drop-2: Take out the second highest note and move it to the lowest position, i.e. change the permutation to pn−1,p1,p2,⋯ ,pn−3,pn−2,pnp_{n-1}, p_1, p_2, \cdots, p_{n-3}, p_{n-2}, p_npn−1​,p1​,p2​,⋯,pn−3​,pn−2​,pn​. Miyako would like to change the permutation to an ordered permutation, 1,2,⋯ ,n1, 2, \cdots, n1,2,⋯,n, in the fewest number of multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops possible. 对于操作 Drop-2\text{Drop-2}Drop-2，可以将 p1∼pn−1p_1\sim p_{n-1}p1​∼pn−1​ 看作一个环，环的长度为 n−1n-1n−1，即进行 n−1n-1n−1 次操作 Drop-2\text{Drop-2}Drop-2，排列还原；对于操作 Invert\text{Invert}Invert，可以将 p1∼pnp_1\sim p_np1​∼pn​ 看作一个环，环的长度为 nnn，即进行 nnn 次操作 Invert\text{Invert}Invert，排列还原。形成的两个环如图所示，√\color{red}\surd√ 代表当前排列 ppp 的第一个数，×\color{red}\times× 代表位于大环（长度为 nnn 的环）上，而在小环（长度为 n−1n-1n−1 的环）外的数。

小环和大环可以独立顺时针转动，两个指针 ×\color{red}\times× 和 √\color{red}\surd√ 的位置是固定不动的；当转动小环，视为进行了一次 multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops，当转动大环，视为进行多次 Invert\text{Invert}Invert。可以发现，当转动小环，可以将位于小环外的数字，插入任意两个数字之间。在图中，当前 666 位于 333 和 222 之间，转动一次小环，666 就在 111 和 333 之间了；显然，进行一次 multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops，可以将 666 放入任何你想要的位置。更一般的，若要该边数字 xxx 在大环中的相对其他数字位置，应该首先用多次 Invert\text{Invert}Invert 将 xxx 放入 ×\color{red}\times× 标记的位置，然后进行一次 multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops。我们要对大环上的数字进行排序，那么只需要通过若干次操作，利用小环调整一些数字的相对位置，令大环上形成 1→2→⋯→n→1→⋯1\to2\to\cdots\to n\to 1\to\cdots1→2→⋯→n→1→⋯ 这样的环，再进行几次 Invert\text{Invert}Invert 即可将原序列 ppp 完成排序。
那么问题就变得简单：每次调整一个数的位置就要进行一次 multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops，因此要调整尽可能少的数字。不妨找出大环上的一个 LIS\text{LIS}LIS（最长上升子序列），其长度为 lenlenlen；对于在 LIS\text{LIS}LIS 上的 lenlenlen 个数字不作调整，只需要调整 n−lenn-lenn−len 个数字的相对位置；显然，这样的方案使得需要调整的数字个数最小。对于不在 LIS\text{LIS}LIS 上的 n−lenn-lenn−len 个数字，用 n−lenn-lenn−len 次 multi-drops\text{multi-drops}multi-drops 和若干次 Invert\text{Invert}Invert，即可将这 n−lenn-lenn−len 个数字一一放入正确的位置，完成排序。
计算环上的 LIS\text{LIS}LIS 长度时，可以枚举环的起点，对 nnn 个起点各求一次 LIS\text{LIS}LIS，取长度最大的即可。时间复杂度为 O(n2log⁡n)O(n^2\log n)O(n2logn)。
